What is Fake News

At its core, we are defining “fake news” as those news stories that are false: the story itself is fabricated, with no verifiable facts, sources or quotes. Sometimes these stories may be propaganda that is intentionally designed to mislead the reader.

Where does it come from?

Who are the authors? They may be:


  • Someone wanting to make money, regardless of the content of the article

  • Satirists who want to either make a point or entertain you, or both

  • Poor or untrained journalists - the pressure of the 24 hour news cycle as well as the explosion of news sites may contribute to shoddy writing that doesn't follow professional journalistic standards or ethics

  • Partisans who want to influence political beliefs and policy maker

What can I do about "fake news"?

Think critically. Use the strategies on these pages to evaluate the likely accuracy of information. Think twice. If you have any doubt, do NOT share the information.
